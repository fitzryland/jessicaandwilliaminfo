You will find yourself with plenty of options and countless guides and recommendations for your down time. If you’d like to experience the New Orleans that we grew to love and cherish, here are our recommendations for a slightly less-traveled version of New Orleans...

Live Music:

Of course, there is no shortage of live music venues in the Crescent City. Here are some of our favorites. Be sure to check out the WWOZ Livewire Music Calendar for the show listings on the night you want to hit the streets.

The Blue Nile is a classic local live music spot on Frenchman Street (the “local French Quarter”). They’re sure to have some loud, funky sounds pouring from their doors.

Snug Harbor provides more of a low-key atmosphere in which to enjoy some of the premiere jazz sets around -- the famous Marsalis family of jazz musicians are regulars.

For you youngsters hungry for a driving beat and thirsty for a cheap drink, check out the Dragon’s Den. Probably not the place to bring your mom, but one of the sweetly sweatiest and adored spots for local kids out for a time.

Cafe Negril, also located in the Marigny/Frenchman Street area, is a great place to check out some reggae.

You know what, just head to Frenchman Street and have a look around. It’s always a good time -- besides, you kind of have to experience Bourbon Street to get there anyway. It’ll be fun. Promise. Just don’t get too bogged down on Bourbon and miss some of the more interesting spots just around the corner.

There’s always Tipitina’s, the Maple Leaf, and Le Bon Temps Roule if you’re uptown in search of music.

Just be sure to save your energy for the official partying on Friday and Saturday nights!

Our Restaurants:

There are limitless restaurant options in this Southern city. On a night to yourselves, check out some of these gems, our absolute favorite, memory-flooding eateries.

Mandina’s Restaurant is a city treasure -- funky, one-of-a-kind, outrageously excellent down-scale food. Best turtle soup there is. Hands down.

In the Quarter, try the Napoleon House’s shrimp remoulade stuffed avocado. It’s ridiculously good, but so is everything else there. A favorite.

Another Quarter option is Bayona, which serves multicultural take on New Orleans food.

For a more up-scale evening out, choose one of the Brennan Family Restaurants. They’re renowned for excellence in fine dining, and each restaurant has it’s own unique character and culinary palate.

Cochon in the Central Business District is a trendy, outstanding spot.

Herbsaint Restaurant is an upscale option just up the street from Cochon in the CBD.

Central Grocery is the home of the original muffaletta sandwich -- delicious.

For a jazz brunch option on Sunday, settle in at the Court of Two Sisters for outrageous food in a sensuous atmosphere.

For a taste of our collegiate dining options, head uptown for Mona’s Cafe and their quick tasty middle eastern food.

Grab a po’boy from William’s favorite hole in the wall, Adam’s Street Grocery. Killer options include fried shrimp, hot sausage, or oyster -- dressed, add hot sauce. Yum!

Casamento’s for some of the best, freshest oysters around. They’re only open during oyster season, so don’t miss your chance.

Also uptown, drop in to Camilla Grill for a rueben and classic New Orleans-style diner food.

Slim Goodies Diner provides a unique take on diner food, with avocado- and plantain-laced deliciousness.

Outdoor Family-Style Fun:

Cafe Du Monde is simply a classic. You cannot visit New Orleans without snacking on a few beignets at this institution of the community. It’s just across Jackson Square from the wedding festivities and it’s open 24 hours, so it’s there for you early risers and late nighters.

The Steamboat Natchez departs daily from behind the Jax Brewery on Decatur Street in the French Quarter.

Hop on the St. Charles Streetcar heading uptown for another classic New Orleans experience. Watch the old mansions drift by as you make your way to the Tulane and Loyola campuses.

Jump off the streetcar to experience Audubon Park, the site of our first date exactly seven years ago to the day of our wedding. While you’re over there, you can check out our college campuses or play a round of golf at the public course.

Head toward the river, past the zoo, and check out “The Fly” -- a haven for tanning and frisbee-throwing and beer-drinking along the Mississippi River levee. It’s perfect for a sunset or sunrise, depending on your state of mind.

City Park was also always a favorite of ours. It’s huge and beautiful and full of interesting little nooks and crannies. The Sculpture Garden is fun and free. The New Orleans Museum of Art and the Botanical Gardens are also in City Park -- less free but just as interesting. Get there on the Canal Street Streetcar line.
